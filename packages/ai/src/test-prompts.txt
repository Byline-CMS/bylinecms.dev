2. ---

Write a structured, analytical summary of current global affairs (focused on 2024–present) through the lens of shifting and increasingly unstable global power dynamics.

Your analysis must focus especially on the role of the United States—including domestic polarization and foreign policy uncertainty, particularly associated with Donald Trump and Trump-aligned political movements—and how this may affect global stability in the coming years.

Address the following three areas:

Global Power Shifts

Identify the most important current geopolitical forces shaping world affairs (e.g., U.S.–China competition, Russia’s behavior, Global South alignment, energy security, AI and defense technologies).

Explain why the current international order is under stress.

Implications for Multilateral Institutions
Assess the near- and medium-term outlook for major multilateral institutions, including:

United Nations (UN)

NATO

World Trade Organization (WTO)
For each, evaluate:

its current legitimacy and operational effectiveness

likely pressures or reforms

risks of fragmentation or weakening

realistic scenarios for evolution over the next 3–10 years

Europe’s Strategic Future
Explain how Europe may adapt to these shifts, including:

defense and security autonomy vs reliance on the U.S.

internal political cohesion (populism, enlargement, democratic resilience)

energy transition and industrial competitiveness

relations with Russia, China, and the U.S.

Output requirements:

Write in a neutral, non-partisan, policy-analysis tone

Use clear headings and bullet points where useful

Distinguish between:

widely accepted facts

plausible interpretations

forward-looking scenarios

Include 3–5 scenarios for the future global order (with short names like “Fragmented Blocs” or “Reformed Multilateralism”), and give each a probability estimate (low/medium/high)

Conclude with key takeaways and early warning indicators to watch over the next 12–24 months

Target length: 900–1,200 words

Avoid sensationalism. Be explicit about uncertainty where appropriate.
